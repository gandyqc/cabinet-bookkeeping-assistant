Privacy Policy

Application: Cabinet Bookkeeping Assistant
Data controller: Monette Fortin Tremblay inc.
Effective date: April 16, 2026
Last updated: April 16, 2026

1. Who we are

Monette Fortin Tremblay inc. (the "Firm", "we", "us", or "our") is a chartered professional accounting firm incorporated under the laws of Québec, Canada. We operate Cabinet Bookkeeping Assistant (the "Application") as an internal tool to serve our clients. This Privacy Policy describes how we collect, use, protect, and disclose information obtained through the Application.

2. Scope of this policy

This policy applies to data that the Application accesses on your behalf from your QuickBooks Online ("QBO") company, after you have authorized the connection through Intuit's OAuth 2.0 consent screen. It does not apply to data you provide directly to Intuit or to any other service.

3. Data we collect from your QBO company

With your explicit OAuth consent, the Application accesses the following categories of data within the scope com.intuit.quickbooks.accounting:

CategoryExamplesWhy we access it
Company informationCompany name, legal address, fiscal year start Identify the company and configure reporting periods
Chart of accountsAccount names, numbers, types, balances Validate postings and generate financial statements
Vendors / CustomersNames, addresses, balances, payment terms Record bills/invoices against the correct parties
TransactionsBills, invoices, journal entries, line items, amounts, dates, tax codes Provide bookkeeping services and reports
ReportsTrial balance, profit and loss, balance sheet, aged receivables/payables Produce periodic financial statements for your files
OAuth tokensEncrypted refresh and access tokens, realm ID Maintain authorized connection between sessions

The Application does not access QBO payroll data, bank-feed credentials, or any data outside the com.intuit.quickbooks.accounting scope.

4. How we use your data

We use the data strictly to perform the bookkeeping services you have engaged us to perform, specifically:

  • reviewing, classifying, and posting transactions to your QBO company;
  • producing periodic financial reports and providing them to you;
  • reconciling accounts (bank, vendor, customer);
  • communicating with you about your bookkeeping and responding to questions.

We do not use your data for advertising, profiling, resale, or any purpose unrelated to the services you have engaged us for.

5. Where your data is stored

The Application runs on servers located at the Firm's offices in Québec, Canada. Data retrieved from QBO is cached in encrypted form on a local area network that is not accessible from the public internet. OAuth tokens are stored on the same infrastructure with restrictive file-system permissions. We do not transfer your data to any third-party cloud service other than Intuit itself.

6. Who has access

Access to the Application is limited to authorized employees of the Firm (chartered professional accountants, bookkeeping technicians, and information-technology staff) who require the data to perform their duties. All employees are bound by the Firm's Code of Ethics and by professional confidentiality obligations under the Québec Chartered Professional Accountants Act (CQLR, c. C-48.1).

7. How long we keep your data

Transactional data cached locally by the Application is retained for the duration of the engagement and for the period required by applicable record-retention rules (in Québec, typically six (6) years following the end of the fiscal period to which the data relates). OAuth tokens are deleted within two (2) business days of the termination of the engagement or of your revocation of the connection.

8. Your rights

Under Québec's Act respecting the protection of personal information in the private sector (as amended by Bill 64 / Law 25) and the federal Personal Information Protection and Electronic Documents Act (PIPEDA), you have the right to:

  • access the personal information we hold about you;
  • request the correction of inaccurate information;
  • request the deletion of your information, subject to our record-retention obligations;
  • withdraw your consent at any time by disconnecting the Application from your QBO company or by contacting us;
  • file a complaint with the Commission d'accès à l'information du Québec or the Office of the Privacy Commissioner of Canada.

9. Security measures

We implement reasonable technical and organizational measures to protect your data, including:

  • TLS encryption for all communications with the Intuit API;
  • OAuth 2.0 with automatic token refresh and a read-only operating mode for diagnostic or review sessions;
  • personally-identifiable-information (PII) masking in internal logs (social insurance numbers, bank account numbers, credit cards are redacted before logging);
  • restrictive file permissions on stored tokens (mode 600, owner-only read/write);
  • role-based access control within the Application;
  • network isolation: the Application is not exposed to the public internet.

10. Disclosure to third parties

We do not sell, rent, or trade your information. We only disclose it to:

  • Intuit, as required to operate the QBO integration;
  • government authorities, when legally compelled by subpoena or court order;
  • our professional insurers or legal counsel in the context of a professional matter, bound by confidentiality.

11. Cookies and tracking

The Application does not use cookies, advertising identifiers, or third-party analytics. This public information site (the page you are reading) is a static website hosted on GitHub Pages and does not track visitors.

12. Children's privacy

The Application is a professional tool and is not directed at children under 14. We do not knowingly collect information from children.

13. Changes to this policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. The effective date at the top of this page reflects the most recent version. Material changes will be communicated to active clients at least thirty (30) days before they take effect.
