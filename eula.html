2. Purpose and scope

The Application is an internal tool used by the Firm's authorized personnel to provide bookkeeping and accounting services to the Firm's clients, including but not limited to:

  • reading the chart of accounts, vendor and customer lists, and balances;
  • generating financial reports (trial balance, profit and loss, balance sheet, receivables/payables aging, transaction listings);
  • creating or correcting purchase bills and sales invoices on the Client's instructions and with the Client's prior authorization;
  • performing bank reconciliations against the Client's accounting records.

The Application is not a public product. It is not distributed, sold, or licensed to any party other than the Firm itself, and is not available on any app store or public marketplace.

Subject to this Agreement, the Firm grants the Client a non-exclusive, non-transferable, revocable, limited right to connect the Client's QBO company to the Application solely for the purposes described in Section 2 and for the duration of the professional services engagement between the Client and the Firm.

The Client may revoke this connection at any time by either:

  • contacting the Firm at Nbrazeau@mftcpa.com, who will disconnect the integration within two (2) business days; or
  • disconnecting the Application directly from the Client's QBO company (Settings → Integrations → Apps → Disconnect).

4. Authorization and scope of access

When the Client authorizes the connection, Intuit grants the Application an OAuth 2.0 access token scoped to com.intuit.quickbooks.accounting. This scope permits read and write access to accounting data in the connected company only. The Firm will only perform write operations (creating bills, invoices, or related entities) upon explicit instruction or pre-established mandate from the Client as part of the professional services engagement.
